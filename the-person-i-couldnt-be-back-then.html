The Person I Couldn't Be Back Then

Published: July 23, 2026

There are days when I look back at the person I used to be, and I struggle to recognize him.

Not because he was a stranger.

But because he was someone who had so much to learn.

When I reflect on my past, I don't want to rewrite it.

I want to tell the truth about it.

I wasn't physically abusive, but I know I caused emotional pain.

I was disrespectful.

I said things I should never have said.

I rubbed mistakes in her face instead of showing compassion.

Looking back now, I realize she deserved a far better version of me than the one she received.

That truth is difficult to live with.

I often think about the man I am today.

I wish she had met him instead.

Back then, I was young.

I wanted to have fun.

I wanted to experience everything my friends were experiencing.

I convinced myself that because I wasn't married, I shouldn't have to feel tied down by commitment.

So I chased excitement instead of character.

I moved from one girl to another.

If I'm being completely honest, I became a serial cheat.

At the time, I didn't fully understand what I was doing.

I didn't understand what commitment meant.

I didn't understand what it meant to love someone well.

I didn't understand that love isn't just a feeling.

It's a decision you make every day.

Today, I see love differently.

When I love someone, I want to understand them.

I want to know what makes them feel safe.

I want to learn their love language.

I want to treat them with patience and respect.

I want to build a future with them.

I think about growing old together.

I think about building a home.

I think about marriage.

I think about raising children together.

Those thoughts weren't real to me back then.

They are now.

Sometimes I wonder what life would have looked like if I had become this version of myself sooner.

Maybe some things would have been different.

Maybe they wouldn't.

I'll never know.

One of the hardest lessons I've had to accept is that growth doesn't erase consequences.

Sometimes the person you've become arrives after you've already hurt someone.

That is a painful reality.

But it's also the reason I refuse to waste what those experiences taught me.

I can't become the man I needed to be yesterday.

I can only choose to become that man today.

So I don't write this to ask for sympathy.

I don't write it to excuse my past.

I write it because I never want to become that person again.

If these lessons can help me love better, live better, and become better, then perhaps they haven't been wasted.

I'm not writing because I've arrived.

I'm writing because I'm still learning.

I'm still learning.

— EPP
